Non-24-Hour Sleep-Wake Disorder

What it is

Non-24-Hour Sleep-Wake Disorder (Non-24) is a circadian rhythm disorder where the body's master clock can't synchronize with the 24-hour solar day. The sleep–wake cycle drifts progressively later, cycling through all 24 clock hours over approximately 17–18 days.

This is not a behavioral pattern. Non-24 cannot be corrected through earlier wake times, light exposure, sleep hygiene protocols designed for conventional insomnia, or willpower. It is a neurological difference in the endogenous clock period — the circadian tau.

How this page is calculated

The 25.40-hour figure is derived from extended free-running intervals — periods with minimal external time cues — recorded via Oura ring. The anchor (most recent confirmed wake time) is pulled live from the Oura API. All forecasts are computed forward from that anchor using the measured tau.

No standard scheduling tool can accurately represent a 25.40-hour timeline. This page exists to make that representation possible.
